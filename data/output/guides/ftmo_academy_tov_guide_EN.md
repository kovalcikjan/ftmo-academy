- Marketing language

## 4. Persona-Specific Adaptations

### Overview

| Element | Beginner | Aspiring Challenger | Retail Refugee |
|---------|----------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Vocabulary** | Simple, always defined | Standard trading terms | Advanced OK |
| **Assumed knowledge** | None | Basic terminology | Solid foundation |
| **Explanation depth** | High (why + how) | Medium (how + example) | Low (straight to point) |
| **Examples** | Very basic | Practical, Challenge-focus | Edge cases |
| **Tone** | Structured instructor | Professional guide | Peer expert |
| **Pacing** | Slow, step by step | Medium | Fast, scannable |

---

### Beginner Adaptation

**Mindset:** *"I know nothing, but I want to learn. I need clear structure and facts."*

**Approach:**
- Start with "why this matters" (business case, not motivation)
- Define EVERY term on first use
- Use specific examples with numbers
- Provide realistic timeframes (facts, not comfort)
- Clear structure: prerequisite → concept → application

**Tone markers:**
- "This lesson covers..."
- "The reason this matters is..."
- "This concept has three components..."
- "Typical learning curve: 3-6 months of consistent practice."

**Avoid:**
- Assumed knowledge
- Quick jumps between concepts
- Technical jargon without explanation
- Emotional reassurance ("don't worry", "you've got this")

---

### Aspiring Challenger Adaptation

**Mindset:** *"I know the basics, but I lack consistency. I want structure and process, not another 'secret strategy'."*

**Approach:**
- Focus on PROCESS, not results
- Practical examples with real numbers
- Frameworks and checklists
- Connection to FTMO rules where relevant
- Acknowledgement of common mistakes

**Tone markers:**
- "Here's the framework..."
- "A common mistake is..."
- "In practice, this looks like..."
- "To apply this to your Challenge..."

**Avoid:**
- Overly basic explanations (loses respect)
- Vague advice without specifics
- Hype about results
- Ignoring psychological aspects

---

### Retail Refugee Adaptation

**Mindset:** *"I know how to trade, I need to know how to pass FTMO. Don't hold me up with basics."*

**Approach:**
- Get straight to the point
- FTMO-specific rules and nuances
- Edge cases and "gotchas"
- Efficiency tips
- Respect for existing knowledge

**Tone markers:**
- "The key difference with FTMO is..."
- "Watch out for..."
- "To optimize your approach..."
- "Advanced consideration:..."

**Avoid:**
- Condescending "beginner" framing
- Repetition of basics
- Long intros before value
- Overly enthusiastic tone

## 5. Language Guidelines

### Vocabulary: Use vs. Avoid

| USE | AVOID | Why |
|-----|-------|-----|
| Consistent | Lucky | Emphasis on skill, not chance |
| Process | Secret | No guru messaging |
| Develop | Natural talent | Skills can be learned |
| Challenging | Easy | Realistic expectations |
| Professional | Amateur | Aspirational identity |
| Framework | Hack | Structure, not shortcuts |
| Risk management | Gambling | Professional approach |
| Skill | Gift | Learnable, not innate |
| Strategy | System | Flexibility |
| Discipline | Willpower | Process, not willpower |

---

### Prohibited Phrases

**Hype & Promises:**
- "Get rich"
- "Easy money"
- "Guaranteed profits"
- "Life-changing income"
- "Financial freedom in X months"
- "Secret strategy"
- "What they don't want you to know"

**Condescending:**
- "It's simple, just..."
- "Even beginners can understand..."
- "Obviously..."
- "As everyone knows..."
- "You should already know..."

**Vague:**
- "Trade smarter"
- "Be disciplined"
- "Manage your risk" (without specifics)
- "Follow your plan" (without explaining how)

---

### Power Phrases (Use These)

**Educational:**
- "Here's how this works in practice..."
- "The key principle is..."
- "A common mistake to avoid..."
- "Let's break this down..."
- "To apply this..."

**Factual Context (not emotional support):**
- "This typically takes 3-6 months of consistent practice..."
- "Statistically, most traders need multiple attempts..."
- "The goal is consistency, measured by [specific metrics]..."
- "Focus on process adherence, not immediate results..."

**FTMO-Specific:**
- "To prepare for your Challenge..."
- "This directly applies to FTMO's rules..."
- "When trading a funded account..."
- "Professional traders approach this by..."

**Credibility:**
- "Research shows..."
- "In our experience with thousands of traders..."
- "The data indicates..."
- "Statistically..."

---

### Sentence Structure

**Length:**
- Average: 15-20 words
- Mix of short (definitions) and medium (explanations)
- Split long sentences into multiple

**Voice:**
- Prefer ACTIVE voice: "Traders use stop losses" not "Stop losses are used by traders"
- Passive voice OK for processes: "Orders are executed at market price"

**Example:**

> **GOOD:** "Risk management protects your capital. Without it, even profitable strategies fail. Here's the framework we recommend..."

> **BAD:** "In the context of trading activities, the implementation of risk management protocols is considered to be of paramount importance for the preservation of trading capital..."

---

### Straight to the Point — No Filler

Every sentence must open with the actual point. Throat-clearing phrases pad word count without adding value and undermine the confident, direct voice.

**Banned opening constructions:**

| Instead of | Write |
|------------|-------|
| "It is important to note that X" | State X directly |
| "It is worth mentioning that X" | State X directly |
| "When it comes to X, ..." | Start with X |
| "In order to X, ..." | "To X, ..." |
| "One of the most important things is..." | Name the thing |
| "There are several reasons why..." | State the reasons |
| "As we have already seen / mentioned..." | Delete — do not recap |
| "With that said, ..." | Delete or restructure |
| "Having said that, ..." | "But" or restructure |
| "Let us now look at / explore..." | Start the section |
| "Before we dive in, ..." | Delete |
| "The fact that X means Y" | "X means Y" |
| "This is because X" | Direct cause-effect sentence |
| "As a result of this, ..." | State the consequence |

**Rule:** If removing the opening phrase leaves the sentence intact and clearer — remove it.

**Why this matters:** Filler phrases create the impression of hedging or padding. The Academy voice is confident and direct. Every sentence should respect the reader's time.

---

### Addressing the Reader

**Primary:** 2nd person "you" (direct, personal)
- "You'll learn how to..."
- "Your goal is to..."

**Secondary:** 1st person plural "we" (inclusive, collaborative)
- "Let's explore..."
- "We'll cover..."

**Tertiary:** 3rd person "traders" (professional framing)
- "Professional traders approach..."
- "Successful traders tend to..."

**AVOID:**
- "One should..." (too formal)
- "The trader must..." (impersonal)
- "Users are expected to..." (corporate)

## 6. Emotional Calibration

### Principle: Competence, Not Empathy

FTMO Academy supports traders by **GIVING THEM TOOLS** - not by validating their feelings. We are practical, not therapeutic.

| RIGHT | WRONG |
|-------|-------|
| "Here's the framework to solve this problem." | "We understand how frustrating this must be for you." |

---

### What We Express

| Emotion | When | How |
|---------|------|-----|
| **Matter-of-fact acknowledgement** | Stating reality | "Most traders experience losses initially. Here's how to manage them." |
| **Confidence** | Presenting frameworks | "This approach works because..." |
| **Caution** | Discussing risks | "Be aware that..." |
| **Respect** | Addressing the reader | Professional treatment, no dumbing down |

---

### What We DON'T Express

| Emotion | Why Not |
|---------|---------|
| **Emotional empathy** | "We know how hard this feels" - not our role |
| **Excitement/Hype** | Undermines credibility |
| **Cheerleading** | "You can do it!" - empty encouragement |
| **Fear-mongering** | Manipulative |
| **Excessive warmth** | We're mentors, not friends |
| **Superiority** | Condescending |

---

### Handling Difficult Topics

**Risk & Loss:**
| DON'T | DO |
|-------|-----|
| "Don't worry, losses are rare if you follow our method!" | "Losses are inevitable in trading. The goal is to manage them so they don't derail your progress. Here's how." |

**Challenge Failure:**
| DON'T | DO |
|-------|-----|
| "You won't fail if you follow these rules!" | "Statistically, many traders require multiple attempts. The key factors for success are: [specific list]." |

**Complexity:**
| DON'T | DO |
|-------|-----|
| "This is simple!" | "This concept has several layers. Let's tackle them one at a time." |

## 7. Risk & Compliance Sensitivity

The tone reflects financial industry caution. This is critical for YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) content.

### Core Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Avoid outcome guarantees** | Never promise profits, funding, or specific results |
| **Frame opportunity, not promise** | "You can apply for..." not "You will receive..." |
| **Use conditional language** | "may", "can", "if objectives are met" |
| **Emphasize evaluation criteria** | Focus on process and requirements |

### Examples

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| "You will earn funding." | "Traders who meet the objectives may receive a funded account." |
| "This strategy guarantees profits." | "This strategy has shown positive results in backtesting." |
| "You'll make money with FTMO." | "FTMO provides capital to traders who demonstrate consistency." |
| "Easy path to funded trading." | "A structured evaluation process for aspiring funded traders." |

### Required Disclaimers

Risk warning and educational notice are injected by the CMS at publish time. Do not add them to article content.

## 8. Quiz & Assessment Tone

### Question Framing

**Good (Testing Understanding):**
> "A trader with a $50,000 account wants to risk 2% per trade. What is their maximum risk in dollars?"

**Avoid (Trick Questions):**
> "Which of these is NOT a type of analysis that is NEVER used by traders who DON'T want to fail?"

---

### Feedback Tone

**Correct Answer:**
> "Correct! [Brief reinforcement of why this is right]"

**Incorrect Answer:**
> "Not quite. [Explanation without shame]. Review [specific section] to strengthen this concept."

## 10. Examples: Before & After

### Example 1: Explaining Risk Management

**BEFORE (Too Casual):**
> So basically, risk management is super important! You don't wanna blow your account, right? LOL. Just don't risk too much and you'll be fine!

**AFTER (Academy ToV):**
> Risk management is the foundation of sustainable trading. Without it, even profitable strategies eventually fail.
>
> The principle is straightforward: limit the amount you can lose on any single trade to a small percentage of your account. Most professional traders risk between 0.5% and 2% per trade.
>
> Here's why this matters: With 2% risk per trade, you'd need 50 consecutive losing trades to lose your entire account. That's nearly impossible with any reasonable strategy. But at 10% risk per trade? Just 10 losses wipes you out - and 10-trade losing streaks happen to everyone.

---

### Example 2: Introducing Technical Analysis

**BEFORE (Too Academic):**
> Technical analysis is a methodology for forecasting the direction of prices through the study of past market data, primarily price and volume. The discipline is predicated on the assumption that market prices reflect all known information and that price patterns tend to repeat over time.

**AFTER (Academy ToV):**
> Technical analysis helps you make trading decisions by studying price charts.
>
> The core idea: price movements aren't random. They follow patterns that repeat because human psychology - fear and greed - doesn't change. By learning to recognize these patterns, you can anticipate where price might go next.
>
> You'll use tools like:
> - Support and resistance levels (where price tends to stop or reverse)
> - Candlestick patterns (visual signals of buyer/seller behavior)
> - Indicators (mathematical calculations that highlight trends or momentum)
>
> Let's start with the most fundamental tool: understanding what candlesticks actually tell you.

---

### Example 3: Addressing a Common Mistake

**BEFORE (Condescending):**
> A lot of beginners make the stupid mistake of not using stop losses. This is Trading 101, people! If you're not using stops, you shouldn't be trading. Period.

**AFTER (Academy ToV):**
> One of the most common mistakes traders make is skipping stop losses.
>
> Why do traders skip them? Two reasons:
> 1. Stops feel like admitting defeat before the trade plays out
> 2. Experience of being stopped out right before price reverses
>
> The data tells a different story: trading without stops isn't a strategy - it's gambling. A single adverse move can eliminate weeks of gains.
>
> Professional traders treat stops as a defined cost of business, not as failure. The question isn't "should I use a stop?" but "where should I place it for this specific setup?"
>
> That's what we'll cover next.

## 11. Quality Checklist

Before publishing any Academy content, verify voice and tone compliance:

### Voice & Tone
- [ ] Sounds like a professional guide, not a textbook or a friend
- [ ] Confident and direct, not warm or emotional
- [ ] No hype, no false promises, no cheerleading
- [ ] Factual about challenges and risks
- [ ] Competence-based support (tools, not empathy)

### Language
- [ ] Key terms defined when first used
- [ ] Sentences are digestible (15-20 words average)
- [ ] Active voice predominates
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] No prohibited phrases (see Section 5)
- [ ] Power phrases used appropriately
- [ ] No filler openers — every sentence starts with the actual point (see "Straight to the Point" in Section 5)

### Persona Fit
- [ ] Appropriate for target persona(s)
- [ ] Assumed knowledge level is correct
- [ ] Pacing matches audience needs
- [ ] Tone markers match persona (see Section 4)

### Emotional Calibration
- [ ] No emotional empathy ("we understand how hard...")
- [ ] No cheerleading or excessive warmth
- [ ] Risks acknowledged matter-of-factly
- [ ] Confidence without hype

### FTMO Alignment
- [ ] Consistent with main brand values
- [ ] No promises or guarantees
- [ ] Connects to FTMO Challenge where relevant
- [ ] Supports professional trader identity

> **Note:** For structure and formatting checks, see [Structure Guide Checklists](ftmo_academy_structure_guide_EN.md#9-checklists)
