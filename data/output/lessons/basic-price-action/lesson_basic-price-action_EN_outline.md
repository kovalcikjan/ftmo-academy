# Basic Price Action Trading (H1)

Key point: Introduce price action as the foundation of technical analysis and map out the five chapters of the lesson.

### Intro (2–3 short paragraphs, 40–70 words each, no H2)

- Paragraph 1: Frame the topic. Price action is the study of how price itself behaves on the chart, without indicators or external signals. It is the baseline skill every trader builds on.
- Paragraph 2: Position this lesson. It is an overview of the core building blocks; each block has its own dedicated chapter.
- Paragraph 3: Set expectations. By the end, the reader knows the vocabulary and the structure of price action analysis, and which chapter to open next.

## What Is Price Action Trading? (H2)

Target keyword: `what is price action trading` (590 vol)

Key points:
- One-sentence definition up front (AIO-friendly): Price action trading is the practice of making trading decisions from raw price movement on the chart, without relying on indicators.
- Why traders use it: price is the final output of every market force. Reading it directly removes a layer of abstraction.
- Scope note: this lesson does not teach patterns or setups. It teaches the context those patterns live in.

*Short paragraph style, 2–3 paragraphs.*

---

## What You Will Learn in This Lesson (H2)

Key points:
- One short paragraph or bullet per chapter (X.Y.Z.), each with: what it covers + why it matters to a trader.
- Five items, mapping to the five chapters of Lekce 2.2.

Structure as a labelled list (bold lead-in + one or two sentences):

1. **Support and resistance** — the price levels where buyers and sellers have historically reacted. Everything else in price action is read in relation to these levels. (Links to 2.2.1.)
2. **Supply and demand zones** — the natural extension of S/R: instead of single lines, you mark the areas where strong moves originated. Useful when a level behaves more like a region than a point. (Links to 2.2.2.)
3. **Trendlines and trend definition** — how to objectively tell whether a market is moving up, down, or sideways, using swing highs and swing lows rather than opinion. (Links to 2.2.3.)
4. **Market environment: ranges and trends** — the state of the market dictates which tools work. The same setup behaves very differently inside a range versus inside a trend. (Links to 2.2.4.)
5. **Activity at levels: pullback, breakout, fakeout** — what actually happens when price reaches a meaningful area. These three behaviours cover most of what you will read on a chart. (Links to 2.2.5.)

Callout: **Keep in Mind** — None of these concepts produces a trade signal on its own. They are lenses that let you describe what the market is doing before you decide whether to act.

## Why Price Action Matters for Traders (H2)

Key points:
- Price action is the substrate under every strategy. Indicator-based systems, pattern systems, even fundamental traders use it to time entries.
- It is the shared language of the chart. Two traders with different strategies can still agree on where support is, or whether the market is trending.
- For Challenge trading in particular, reading context reduces avoidable errors: trading a pullback as a breakout, or fading a trend that is still intact.

*2 short paragraphs.*

## How to Approach This Lesson (H2, optional)

Key points:
- Read the chapters in order. The structure is cumulative: levels → zones → trend → environment → behaviour at levels.
- You do not need to master each chapter before moving on. A working understanding is enough; the chapters reinforce each other.
- Prerequisite: Lesson 2.1. (Charts and timeframes) — you should be comfortable reading a candlestick chart on a single timeframe before starting here.

Closing paragraph / CTA: Start with Chapter 2.2.1. — Support and resistance.
