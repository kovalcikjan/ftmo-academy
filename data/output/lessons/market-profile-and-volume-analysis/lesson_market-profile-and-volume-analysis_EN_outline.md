## Differentiation

- **Positions Market Profile + Volume Profile + Order Flow as one tool family**, not as competing tools. Competitor pages either compare them head-to-head or cover only one in isolation. No trading academy publishes a concise "family roadmap" that shows how the chapters fit together.
- **Frames the common foundation: auction market theory.** All three tools answer the same underlying question — "where did the most trading happen, and why does that matter?" — but view the market through different lenses (time, volume, order flow).
- **Realistic about limitations.** Volume-based tools show where trading happened, not where it will happen. The Lekce sets that expectation upfront so the chapter articles can build on it.
- **Deliberately excluded from this Lekce:** TPO mechanics, Value Area calculation details, platform-specific indicator configuration, strategy-level applications — those belong in the chapter articles.

**Key point for the reader:** This lesson introduces a family of volume-based analytical tools — Market Profile, Volume Profile, and Order Flow — and explains why each matters for reading trader behaviour on a chart.

## Why volume-based analysis matters for traders (H2)

Target keyword: volume analysis

Key points (short paragraphs, 40–70 words each):
- Standard price charts show *where* price moved. Volume-based tools show *where market participants actually committed capital* — a different and often more informative signal.
- The practical edge: identifying zones where the market has already "accepted" a price (high-volume) versus zones it sprinted through without conviction (low-volume). These zones often behave differently when revisited.
- Frame honestly: these tools describe past behaviour. They do not predict the future. Their value is as a lens on participant behaviour, combined with other analysis — not as a standalone trading signal.

Callout: **Keep in Mind** — one callout, framing the "past behaviour, not prediction" limitation. This is the only callout in the Lekce (per simplified Lekce rule).
