## What Is a Timeframe in Trading? (H2)

Key points:
- Definition: period each candlestick/bar represents
- Range: from 1-minute to monthly
- Same market, different perspectives
- Timeframe ≠ holding period (clarify common confusion)

## Trading Styles and Their Timeframes (H2)

Key point: Each style has a natural timeframe range. Match first, refine later.

### Scalping (H3)

- 1-min to 15-min charts
- Dozens of trades per day, seconds-to-minutes holding
- Requires: constant screen time, fast execution, low spreads
- Risk: highest noise, most false signals

Callout: Warning — scalping demands full-time attention and fast decision-making

### Day Trading (H3)

Target keyword: best time frame for day trading

- 5-min to 1-hour charts
- All positions closed by end of session
- Typical: 15min for structure, 5min for entries, 1H for bias
- Risk: intraday volatility, overtrading temptation

### Swing Trading (H3)

Target keyword: best time frame for swing trading

- 4-hour to daily charts
- Holding period: days to weeks
- Less screen time, larger stop losses, smaller position sizes
- Best starting point for part-time traders

### Position Trading (H3)

- Daily to monthly charts
- Holding period: weeks to months
- Fundamental analysis plays bigger role
- Widest stops, smallest positions relative to account

## How to Choose the Right Timeframe (H2)

Target keyword: best time frame to trade forex for beginners

Key point: Decision framework — a process, not a list.

- Factor 1: Available time (full-time vs. part-time vs. occasional)
- Factor 2: Personality (patience, stress tolerance, decision speed)
- Factor 3: Risk tolerance (wider TF = wider stops = different sizing)
- Factor 4: Experience level — beginners start with daily, move down

Callout: Tip — Start with the daily chart. It filters noise and builds discipline.

---

## Timeframes and Risk Management (H2)

Key point: Timeframe directly affects stop-loss distance and position size.

- Shorter TF = tighter stops = larger position possible BUT more noise
- Longer TF = wider stops = smaller position BUT cleaner signals
- Position sizing formula connection (link to Risk Management lesson)
- Example: same account, same risk %, different TF → different lot size

Callout: Important — Your timeframe choice changes your position size. Never increase risk % to compensate for wider stops.

---

## Timeframes in the FTMO Challenge (H2)

Key point: FTMO-specific considerations that competitors don't cover.

- Daily loss limit (5%) — shorter TFs accumulate losses faster
- Maximum loss limit (10%) — position sizing must account for TF
- Time pressure (evaluation period) — balance between opportunity and discipline
- Most successful FTMO traders use 1H–Daily range

Callout: Tip — In the FTMO Challenge, consistency matters more than frequency. A swing trading approach on the 4H/Daily often outperforms aggressive scalping.

---

## Common Mistakes (H2)

- Switching timeframes mid-trade (chasing confirmation)
- Using too short a TF for experience level
- Ignoring higher TF context
- Changing TF after a losing streak instead of reviewing strategy

Callout: Keep in Mind — The urge to drop to a lower timeframe after losses is emotional, not analytical.
