# Indicators and Oscillators (H1)

**Key point:** Introduce indicators and oscillators as a family of tools, then signpost the five chapters that teach the family member by member.

---

## Intro (no heading — opening paragraphs)

**Paragraph 1 (opening, AIO-friendly definition, 40–60 words):**
Define indicators and oscillators in one sentence. Indicators are mathematical calculations applied to price or volume, displayed either on top of the chart or in a separate window. Oscillators are a subset of indicators that fluctuate within a bounded range, typically used to read momentum and overbought / oversold conditions.

**Paragraph 2 (the visual distinction, 40–60 words):**
The simplest way traders remember the difference: indicators sit directly on the price chart (moving averages, Bollinger Bands), while oscillators sit below it in their own pane (RSI, MACD, Stochastics). This lesson covers both families in five focused chapters.

## Why Indicators and Oscillators Matter for Traders (H2)

Target keyword: oscillators in trading

**Paragraph (50–70 words):**
Reading a raw candlestick chart gives you price. Indicators and oscillators compress that price into a second layer of information: trend direction, momentum, volatility, and exhaustion. Used well, they filter noise and define entries and exits. Used poorly, they produce false confidence and late signals. The difference is understanding what each tool measures and what it cannot see.

**Callout: Keep in Mind (required for TA lessons) — 1 short sentence:**
> **Keep in Mind:** No indicator predicts the market. They describe what price has already done in a specific mathematical way — the judgment about what happens next is still yours.
