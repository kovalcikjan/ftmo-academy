## Why Fibonacci and Elliott Waves matter for traders (H2)

Target keyword context: "fibonacci retracement", "elliott wave principle"

Key points:
- Explain the role of proportion-based analysis in trading: markets move in legs, and traders need a way to estimate how far a pullback or extension is likely to travel.
- Fibonacci levels answer "where might this stop?" Elliott wave counts answer "where are we in the larger move?" Together they sharpen entries and targets, but either works alone.
- One short paragraph on the honest limitation: these tools do not predict the future. They organise probabilities, and they fail when used without confirmation from price action or volume.

No table. No bullet list (prose only). One paragraph, 60–90 words.

---

## How to approach this module (H2)

Key points:
- Suggested order: read Fibonacci Trading first, Elliott Wave Theory Basics second. Fibonacci is a prerequisite concept for understanding Elliott wave proportions.
- Prerequisites: basic chart reading (Part 2.1), candlestick anatomy (Part 2.2), trend identification (Part 2.3). No prior exposure to indicators is required.
- Practice suggestion: apply Fibonacci on a single currency pair for a week before attempting to count waves. Isolate the tool, build the habit, then layer on the second method.

Short paragraph + 2–3 bullets (compact).

Closing paragraph / CTA (40–60 words)

- Summarise the mental model in one sentence: Fibonacci measures proportions inside a single move, Elliott wave measures proportions across a sequence of moves.
