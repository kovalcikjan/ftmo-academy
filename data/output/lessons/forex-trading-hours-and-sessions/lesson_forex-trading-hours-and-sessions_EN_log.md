### Competitor Content Analysis

**Common structure across top competitors:**
- 4 sessions (Sydney, Tokyo, London, New York) with GMT/EST/local times
- Session overlap windows (London-NY most emphasised)
- Average pip movement tables by session (cityindex, babypips use identical data)
- DST impact section
- Best/worst times to trade
- FAQ section (admiralmarkets, dukascopy)

**Unique angles found:**
- **AvaTrade:** Pip ranges per pair per session, price gap analysis at session opens, DST staggering explanation
- **OANDA (volume):** 70,000 data points, 37% of volume in 19% of trading day (London-NY overlap), per-pair volume breakdowns
- **Dukascopy:** Worst hours for trading, Friday afternoon risk, August liquidity drought
- **Capital.com:** Night trading as a niche strategy, Asian pairs for overnight traders
- **BabyPips (best days):** Day-of-week volatility table, Friday half-day pattern

**Word count range:** 1,100 (OANDA volume) to 3,000+ (AvaTrade, BabyPips)

### Content Gap Summary

1. **No competitor connects sessions to trading style in depth.** Most mention "scalpers prefer London" in one sentence but do not explain which session characteristics match which style (day trading, swing, position).
2. **Volume data is rare.** Only OANDA provides actual volume percentage breakdowns. Most competitors describe "high volume" qualitatively without data.
3. **Spread behaviour across sessions is absent.** No competitor shows how spreads widen/narrow across sessions, despite this being directly relevant to trading costs.
4. **Weekend gaps and Monday open dynamics are underexplored.** Only AvaTrade mentions price gaps at session opens; none cover Sunday/Monday gap risk systematically.
5. **Connection to economic calendar is weak.** Competitors mention "news releases" but do not map specific high-impact releases to their typical session windows.
